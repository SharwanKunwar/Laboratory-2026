# Unit 2: Planning - Important 5 Marks Questions & Answers

## 1. Explain the process of identifying and selecting system development projects.
**Answer:**  
System development projects are identified and selected through the following steps:
1. **Identify project ideas** from problems, opportunities, and directives.  
2. **Evaluate feasibility** of each idea (technical, economic, operational).  
3. **Select the best project** based on business value, cost-benefit, and alignment with organizational goals.

---

## 2. What are the major sources of systems development project requests? Explain.
**Answer:**  
Major sources include:
- **Problems:** Issues in current systems (e.g., manual errors).  
- **Opportunities:** New ideas to improve business (e.g., mobile app).  
- **Directives:** Policies, regulations, or mandates from management or government.

---

## 3. Define Information Systems Planning (ISP). Why is it important?
**Answer:**  
**ISP** is the long-term planning of IT systems to align with business goals.  
**Importance:**  
- Ensures IT supports business strategy.  
- Helps prioritize projects.  
- Avoids wastage of resources and redundant systems.

---

## 4. Explain the criteria used for selecting a systems development project.
**Answer:**  
Criteria include:  
1. **Business Value:** Does it increase profit or reduce cost?  
2. **Technical Feasibility:** Do we have the technology and skills?  
3. **Organizational Feasibility:** Will users accept it?  
4. **Economic Feasibility:** Cost-benefit analysis.

---

## 5. What is project initiation? Explain the activities performed.
**Answer:**  
**Project Initiation** is the official start of a project.  
**Activities:**  
- Define project scope  
- Identify stakeholders  
- Set project objectives  
- Assign project manager

---

## 6. Explain the steps involved in planning a systems development project.
**Answer:**  
1. Define **what** will be done (scope and objectives).  
2. Define **how** it will be done (methods, tools).  
3. Assign **who** will do it (team and responsibilities).  
4. Define **when** it will be done (timeline and schedule).  
5. Estimate **costs** (budget).  
6. Identify **risks** and mitigation plan.

---

## 7. What is feasibility analysis? Explain its different types.
**Answer:**  
**Feasibility Analysis** checks if a project can succeed.  
**Types:**  
- **Technical Feasibility:** Is the technology available?  
- **Economic Feasibility:** Are costs justified by benefits?  
- **Operational Feasibility:** Will users accept the system?  
- **Schedule Feasibility:** Can it be completed on time?

---

## 8. What is a Baseline Project Plan (BPP)? What are its main components?
**Answer:**  
**BPP** is a formal document outlining the project’s plan and serves as a reference.  
**Components:**  
- Project scope and objectives  
- Budget estimation  
- Timeline/schedule  
- Feasibility study results  
- Risk assessment  
- Resource allocation

---

## 9. Explain the importance of planning in systems development projects.
**Answer:**  
Planning ensures:  
- Clear project goals and objectives  
- Efficient use of resources  
- Proper time and cost management  
- Reduced project risks  
- Provides a reference for monitoring progress

---

## 10. Differentiate between project identification and project initiation.
| Feature | Project Identification | Project Initiation |
|---------|----------------------|------------------|
| Purpose | Find possible projects | Officially start selected project |
| Focus | Problems, opportunities, directives | Scope, objectives, stakeholders |
| Stage | Early phase | After selection phase |
| Output | List of potential projects | Baseline Project Plan (BPP) |
