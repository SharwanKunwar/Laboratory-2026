# Statistics
Statistics is the branch of mathematics that deals with collecting, organizing, presenting, analyzing, and interpreting data. It helps in understanding patterns, making decisions, and drawing meaningful conclusions in various fields.

# SPSS
SPSS (Statistical Package for the Social Sciences) is a powerful software tool used for data management and statistical analysis. It helps researchers perform complex calculations, create charts, and interpret data easily and efficiently.

# Data
Data are raw facts and figures collected from observations, experiments, or surveys. They can be numbers, measurements, or descriptions, and are used as the basis for analysis, decision-making, and drawing conclusions.

# Variable
A variable is any characteristic, quality, or attribute that can take different values in a study. Examples include age, height, income, or marks. Variables are essential in statistical analysis to study relationships and patterns.

# Mean
Mean is a measure of central tendency that represents the average value of a dataset. It is calculated by adding all the values and dividing the total by the number of observations, giving a general idea of the data.

# Median
Median is the middle value in a dataset when the data are arranged in ascending or descending order. If the number of values is even, it is calculated as the average of the two middle values.

# Mode
Mode is the value that appears most frequently in a dataset. It helps identify the most common observation and is especially useful in understanding patterns in categorical or numerical data.
